MEPS

Medical Expenditure Panel Survey (MEPS)

MEPS, administered by the Agency for Health Research and Quality (AHRQ), is a set of large scale surveys of families and individaulus, their medical providers (physicians, hospitals, pharmacies, etc.), and employers across the U.S. MEPS collects data on the specific health services that Americans use such as how frequently they use them, the cost of these services, and how they are paid for, as well as data on the cost, scope, and breadth of health insurance held by and available to U.S. workers.

MEPS have three components: Househodl Component, Insurance/Employer Component, and Medical Provider Component. Household Component provides data from individual households and their members, which is supplemented by data from their medical providers. Insurance Component is a separate survey of employers that provides data on employer-based health insurance. Only the Household component is available for download on the internet, and provides comprehensive national estimates of health care use and payment by individuals, families, and any other demographic group of interest.

MEPS uses the National Health Interview Survey (NHIS) sampled population to form its own sampling frame by sampling one-half of NHIS respondent households. After filling out a confidentiality agreement, AHRQ can also be linked to death certificates in the National Death Index. MEPS is an excellent data source for researchers, health expenditures, utilization, payment sources, health status, and insurance coverage and to examine how individuals and their families interavt with the medical car system. BQHS keeps MEPS data and crosswalk data to link to NHIS form 1996 to the present.
