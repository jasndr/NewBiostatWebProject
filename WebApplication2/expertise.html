BQHS Expertise

The faculty and staff at BQHS have extensive experience with epidemiologic investigations, basic science research, clinical studies, clinical trials, large-scale omics data analysis, and community-based investigations. We are ready to provide prompt, high quality biostatistical and bioinformatical services listed below.

Download BQHS Collaboration & Service Brochure

Research Design

  • Grant proposal development and review
  • Research feasibility discussion to establish testable research hypothesis
  • Sample size and statistical power determination
  • Database design
  • Data-instrument development, such as questionaire
  • Data-coding protocols
  • Sampling procedures

Note: To help increase the likelihood of success in obtaining funding, BQHS faculty and staff need sufficient time to customize the research design and data analysis sections for your individual research proposal. We strongly recommend that you contact BQHS as early as possible, at least several weeks before the deadline. The involvement of experienced BQHS faculty and staff from the beginning of the development of a research project tends to significantly increase the quality of an investigation.

Data Analysis

Biostatistics

  • Database design and development (OpenClinica, REDCap, ACCESS)
  • Data coding and management
  • Comprehensive univariable and multivariable analysis
  • Data safety monitoring and interim analysis
  • Tailored statistical tool development to address specific research questions and challenges

Bioinformatics

  • Next Generation Sequencing
    • RNA-Seq
    • DNA-Seq
    • Chip-Seq
    • Exome sequencing
    • Targeted sequencing
    • Variant detection and more
  • Microarray data analysis
  • Proteomics data analysis
  • Gene annotation
  • Pathway Analysis

Results Dissemination

  • Statistical results interpretation
  • Results presentations (e.g., figures and tables) for manuscripts and posters
  • Biostatsitics and bioinformatics methodology and results sections for reports and manuscripts

Note: Authorship is generally expected on scientific journal articles or presentations, where substative input and effort on research design and/or data analysis are provided. Reimbursement for BQHS support (either through %FTE or fee-for-service) does not preclude or replace authorship.

Training and Education

  • Quantitative health science workships and seminars for trainees (graduate students, residents, fellows), faculty, and staff
  • Statistics and bioinformatics courses of various levels of difficulty
